Alcoholic liver steatosis

Steatosis is an obese liver that occurs most often with overweight, diabetes, and alcoholic liver damage. When necrosis and inflammation occur against the background of steatosis, it is steatotic (steatotic) hepatitis.

Most often, steatosis occurs without noticeable symptoms, there may only be discomfort in the abdomen or heaviness in the right hypochondrium. At the same time, the liver is enlarged.

Steatotic hepatitis can be asymptomatic, while at the same time representing a severe liver disease that progresses to liver failure. Ultrasound is the main method of detecting liver steatosis. It shows the so-called "shiny" liver.

In medicine, there is a term "alcoholic liver disease", which combines all the morphological and clinical forms of alcoholic destruction livers.

Alcohol is one of the most harmful substances for the liver. This is the second most common cause of cirrhosis after viral hepatitis. The risk of alcohol damage exists with daily consumption of ethanol, but of course not every person who drinks alcohol will get sick with this disease disease (about 30% of alcohol users are affected).

The following risk factors are important: long-term use of large amounts of alcohol, constant abuse, genetic factors, poor quality of alcohol, female sex, concomitant hepatitis b or C viruses, and others. Mechanism, through which alcohol damages the liver, it is complex and involves the production and accumulation of free radicals, the aggravation of metabolic disorders, the stimulation of collagen synthesis, which leads to the appearance of fibrosis.

Systematic and long-term use of alcohol causes harm to other organs, as well as changes the functioning of the nervous system. personality. Non-specific symptoms may occur, such as fatigue, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, and unpleasant sensations in the upper right half of the body.

Alcoholic steatosis is the most common and first liver damage. This can be asymptomatic manifestations or some other non-specific symptoms. An early-stage examination reveals an increase in the liver, an increase in the level of GGT (gamma-glutamyltransferase). A biopsy is usually not required to make a diagnosis; laboratory tests and ultrasound are sufficient. If a person stops drinking alcohol, adheres to an appropriate diet and takes b vitamins, steatosis should disappear in 4-6 weeks.

Alcoholic hepatitis is an independent disease or is combined with steatosis, fibrosis and cirrhosis of the liver. It is registered in 20-75% of alcoholics. With complicated alcoholic hepatitis, people are in a serious General condition, they have a deficit vitamins, diarrhea, weight loss, possible fever.

Yellowing of the skin appears, the liver is enlarged and painful, ascites, heart rhythm disorders, hypoglycemia are possible, with the risk of developing coma, kidney failure, infections, pneumonia, tuberculosis and others. Hepatic decompensation usually occurs it occurs after a period of intoxication. Significantly increased indicators of GGT, AST, IgA, white blood cells, there are violations of the electrolyte balance. Alcoholic hepatitis is a serious disease, and severe acute hepatitis is life-threatening. The death rate reaches 50% regardless of treatment.

Treatment of alcohol-related illness the liver begins with stopping drinking for the rest of your life. At the same time, attention is paid to psychological problems.

Any cessation of chronic alcohol use is accompanied by a withdrawal syndrome, which is controlled with the help of psychotropic helpers, vitamins, saline solution injections and glucose. Treatment of acute alcoholic hepatitis consists of General therapeutic measures-restoration of electrolyte balance, nutritional and vitamin deficiencies. In severe acute alcoholic hepatitis, specific therapy is used - corticosteroids and others. Antioxidant helpers are also used. Treatment it is performed only under the supervision of a medical specialist.

Foods that help with bloating

The formation of gases in the stomach is unpleasant, especially when the process is intense and painful. Frequent gas formation causes bloating and discomfort that disrupts the rhythm of life.

For finding out causes of bloating can be compiled so-called "cleansing diet". It will help you understand which foods cause gas and frequent bloating. To begin with, exclude products that are considered the main culprits of gas, namely: Brussels sprouts, broccoli, legumes, corn, potatoes, high-fat foods fat content and processed foods.

Do this gradually over a few days. Wait at least a week to see the results, and start adding these foods to your diet again. This is the easiest way to find out which foods present problems and not eat them often.

What foods help with bloating?

To give up all the above-mentioned products is not the best solution, since most of them are endowed with valuable nutritional qualities. The best option is to combine them with various spices that have the ability to reduce stomach discomfort - among them are star anise, cardamom, cinnamon, coriander, cumin, garlic, ginger, nutmeg, parsley and mint.

Peppermint tea is particularly good for digestive problems and bloating. In the same way, a glass of warm water with lemon, which is a natural diuretic, can be useful it also helps to remove excess salt from the body and reduce the feeling of bloating.

Many vegetables work the same way - cucumbers, celery, spinach, and parsley help reduce fluid retention. Consume them after a light heat treatment to reduce their fiber content and ease them digestion.

Regularly eat foods rich in probiotics to improve the condition of the intestinal flora and the balance of bacteria in the digestive system.

And don't forget - real food is fresh, locally produced and without the use of harmful ingredients and chemicals.

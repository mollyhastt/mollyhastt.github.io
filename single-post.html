Which foods are suitable and which are not for pancreatitis?

Pancreatitis is a serious and dangerous disease characterized by inflammation of the pancreas (pancreas) - a glandular organ that produces insulin and other digestive enzymes. Generally patients with pancreatitis experience severe pain when eating, so intravenous injections of drugs and fluids are recommended.

After the inflammation gradually subsides and the patient feels better, food intake is allowed again inside. Then you start eating low-fat food under supervision doctor's.

Following a diet is especially important not only for recovery from pancreatitis, but also for preventing relapses and chronicling the disease.

The goal of making a diet after pancreatitis is to reduce the risk of malnutrition, lack of important nutrients, and optimize blood sugar levels (USC).

Most experts advise that the diet should consist of high-quality proteins, wholegrains, fruits, vegetables, and low-fat foods. Fried foods and foods high in preservatives should be avoided. Even patients are forbidden to drink alcohol and smoke.

On in fact, the diet that doctors recommend to patients recovering from pancreatitis is very close to the so-called Mediterranean diet, more precisely to foods that people in countries such as Greece, Spain and Italy consume routinely. Numerous studies have shown that this diet helps reduce the risk of type 2 DIABETES and liver cancer, protects against Alzheimer's disease, CVD, improves mood and memory.

The traditional Mediterranean diet may seem heavy in some respects in terms of fat content for patients with pancreatitis, but it can easily be changed. In fact, even healthy fats such as olive oil, coconut oil or fish oil, may be too heavy for people with this disease. As with most diseases, the most important thing here is to listen to the signals that the body itself gives, and to recognize foods that make you feel good and do not overload the digestive tract.

By according to experts, the daily menu should be as follows:

- three meals a day from whole grains;

- doubled the consumption of fruit;

- three to four servings of vegetables;

- single use of nuts;

- one serving of lean meat;

- one portion a low-fat dairy product.

What foods should I avoid?

- alcohol, caffeine and nicotine;

- allergens such as wheat, soy, artificial sweeteners;

- fried food;

- white flour products such as bread, pasta, pizza;

products with a high content of preservatives'.
